The Five Pillars

The framework divides initiatives into four sequential stages (P1-P4) and one infrastructural layer (P5) that enables transmission and feedback.

Pillar 1

Entity

The initiating actor (individual, organization, company, government). Defines the mandate, resources, and vision.

Explore P1
Pillar 2

Execution

The implementing body (partner, agency, team, volunteers). The operational arm translating vision to action.

Explore P2
Pillar 3

Initiative

The action, program, project, or intervention. The concrete activity being performed.

Explore P3
Pillar 4

Impact

Measurable change in quality of life, well-being, or capability. The ultimate goal of the entity's action.

Explore P4
Pillar 5 (The Bridge)

Communication

The infrastructural connective layer — THE BRIDGE. Communication enables transmission (P1 → P4), coordination, accessibility, perception, accountability, and feedback (P4 → P1).

Explore Communication (P5)
